Self Confidence.

What is the relationship between mindfulness and self-awareness?

Self-awareness is a by-product of mindfulness. What was most interesting about this chapter, was how self-confidence can be improved through increased levels of self-awareness. I've struggled with self-confidence for a long time, so this chapter really spoke to me.

I tried a practice called 'body scanning'...how did I find it?

Body scanning a surreal experience. I've always known that I hold most of my tension in my stomach muscles. But during the meditation, I realised I also tense my jaw when I'm anxious. This is something I wasn't aware of until this practice. Focusing on my body within during meditation, also helped me stay in the mindful zone for longer.

What did I take away from the 'My Emotions not me' section?

It's easy to be completely mentally overwhelmed by emotions. When this happens we experience our relationship to the world through emotion. This isn't always a helpful way to go through life. The more we do this, the more challenging it can be to detach from difficult situations and gain a clearer perspective. In this chapter, Meng encourages us to gain a level of separation between our mental activity and the chemical emotions we experience. One way to do this is through meditation when emotions arise during mindfulness practices we should take a step back and think aloud 'I'm experiencing anger', etc. This invokes a level of self-awareness and control over our emotional state.
