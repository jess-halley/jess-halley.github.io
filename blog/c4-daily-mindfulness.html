Mindfulness.

What did you learn from your 'mindfulness conversation'? How did the other person find it?

For me, the most interesting component of Meng's 'mindfulness conversation' techniques was the process of 'dipping'. Meng highlights how in regular, everyday conversation we often don't give our full attention to listening to others. This is because it's easy to get distracted by internal feelings and chatter. Meng suggests the best way to respond to this process is to acknowledge this internal chatter, as simply that, internal chatter. When we acknowledge it, it is no longer a distraction. Freeing up your energy to focus on listening to the other person. The key is to acknowledge your internal chatter without judgement. I really liked Meng's analogy of adopting the mindset of a loving grandmother, where instead of judging oneself for internal distractions, you acknowledge these distractions and gently nudge the mind back on track.
